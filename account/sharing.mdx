Every song you create on Suno has its own page and a unique shareable URL, making it easy to get your music in front of an audience — whether that's a single friend or the entire internet.

## Song URLs

Every song lives at a permanent URL in the format `suno.com/song/[id]`. This link works for anyone, even people without a Suno account, as long as the song is public (or you've shared the link directly for a private song).

To copy a song's link:

1. Open the **More Actions (...)** menu on any song in your Library or on the Explore feed.
2. Select **Share**.
3. Choose **Copy Link** to copy the URL to your clipboard.

## Public vs. Private: Controlling Discovery

Your song's privacy setting determines whether other users can find it organically on Suno.

| Setting | Visibility |
|---|---|
| **Public** | Appears on the Explore feed; discoverable by all Suno users |
| **Private** | Hidden from Explore; only you can see it in your Library |

### Making a Song Private After Publishing

If you've already published a song publicly and want to pull it back:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the song">
    Go to your **Library** and locate the song you want to make private.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open More Actions">
    Click the **...** menu on the song.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Privacy">
    Select **Set Privacy** and switch it to **Private**. The song will be removed from the Explore feed immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Even after setting a song to Private, anyone who already has the direct URL can still listen to it. If you need to fully restrict access, delete the song.
</Note>

## Sharing to Social Media

### From the Web App

Use the **Share** option in the **More Actions (...)** menu to share directly to:

- **X (Twitter)** — posts a link with the song title
- **Facebook** — shares the song page link to your feed

### From the Mobile App

The Suno mobile app lets you share audio directly to apps that accept audio files, including:

- **Instagram** (Reels, Stories)
- **TikTok**
- Any other app on your device that accepts audio sharing

## Embedding Songs on Websites

You can embed a Suno song as an audio player directly on any website or blog.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Share menu">
    Click **More Actions (...)** on the song, then select **Share**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the embed code">
    Select the **Embed** option to copy the iframe embed code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste into your site">
    Paste the embed code into your website's HTML editor. The song will appear as a playable audio widget.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The song must be set to **Public** for the embedded player to work for all visitors. A private song's embed will only play for you when you're logged in.
</Tip>

## The Explore Feed

Public songs are eligible to appear on the **Explore** feed, where Suno users discover new music. Highly popular or trending songs may also be featured on the Suno homepage.

To maximize your song's discoverability:

- Set the song to **Public** via the More Actions menu.
- Use a descriptive title so your song is identifiable.
- Engage with the community by liking and commenting on other creators' songs.

## Sharing Your Suno Profile

<Tip>
  Share your Suno profile page to give people a single destination for all your public music. Your profile URL is `suno.com/@[username]`. Anyone visiting your profile can browse and play all your public songs at once.
</Tip>
