Whether you're releasing music on streaming platforms, scoring a short film, or running ads, knowing exactly what you're licensed to do with your Suno songs matters. Your commercial rights depend on the plan you were subscribed to **at the time you created each song**.

## Rights by Plan

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Free Plan" icon="ban">
    **No commercial use rights.** Songs generated on the Free plan are for personal, non-commercial use only. You may not monetize, sell, license, or use them in commercial contexts.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Pro & Premier Plans" icon="check">
    **Full commercial use rights.** Songs generated while you hold an active Pro or Premier subscription are yours to use commercially, royalty-free.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What "Commercial Use" Means

On Pro and Premier, you can use your Suno songs in a wide range of commercial contexts, including:

- **Monetized YouTube videos** — videos that earn ad revenue
- **Podcasts** — both free and paid/subscription-based podcasts
- **Advertisements** — TV, radio, online, and social media ads
- **Films and video production** — short films, documentaries, branded content
- **Social media content** — monetized accounts and sponsored posts
- **Album releases** — release songs on Spotify, Apple Music, Bandcamp, and other platforms
- **Sync licensing** — license your songs for use in third-party videos, games, or other media
- **Selling tracks** — sell your songs on music marketplaces or directly to clients

<Note>
  Commercial rights cover **your use** of the generated song. They do not permit you to resell access to Suno's generation service itself or to offer AI music generation to others on Suno's behalf.
</Note>

## Royalty-Free: What That Means for You

Songs created on Pro or Premier are **royalty-free**. This means:

- You do **not** owe Suno a fee each time your song is played, streamed, or synced.
- You do **not** pay per-use fees when your song appears in a film, ad, or broadcast.
- You pay only your subscription (or a one-time sale price if you sell the track) — not ongoing royalties to Suno.

Suno retains **no ownership** over songs you generate on a paid plan. Once you create a song, it's yours.

## Attribution

Crediting Suno is **not required**. You're free to release songs without any attribution to Suno.

That said, if you'd like to acknowledge the tool, you can optionally include a credit such as *"Made with Suno"* in your video description, liner notes, or social post. It's entirely your choice.

## What Happens If You Cancel or Downgrade

Your commercial rights are tied to the plan active **at the moment of creation**, not your current plan.

- **Songs created while on Pro or Premier** retain their commercial rights permanently — even after you cancel or downgrade to Free.
- **Songs created after downgrading to Free** do not carry commercial use rights, regardless of your subscription history.

<Note>
  Keep a record of your subscription status and the approximate dates you created songs you plan to use commercially. A billing confirmation email or account history page can serve as documentation if you ever need to verify your rights.
</Note>

## Terms of Service

Commercial rights are subject to Suno's [Terms of Service](https://suno.com/terms). The Terms outline additional restrictions, including limitations on generating content that infringes on third-party intellectual property, violates applicable laws, or falls under prohibited content categories. Review the Terms before any significant commercial use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I release a Suno song on Spotify?">
    Yes — if you created the song while subscribed to **Pro or Premier**, you have full commercial rights and can distribute the song to any streaming platform, including Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, and others. Use a music distribution service (such as DistroKid, TuneCore, or CD Baby) to handle the upload and metadata. Note that some distributors have their own policies on AI-generated content, so review their terms before submitting.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to credit Suno?">
    No. Attribution is entirely optional on all paid plans. You can release songs under your own name, a band name, or any other artist identity without mentioning Suno. If you want to acknowledge the platform, feel free to add something like *"Created with Suno AI"* in your notes — but it's never a requirement.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if I cancel my Pro subscription?">
    Songs you generated **while your Pro subscription was active** keep their commercial rights permanently — canceling does not revoke rights from already-created songs. However, any songs you generate after your subscription lapses (on the Free plan) will not carry commercial use rights. To continue creating commercially licensed music, keep an active Pro or Premier subscription.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use Suno songs in ads?">
    Yes, on Pro and Premier. You can use your songs in online ads, social media ads, TV commercials, radio spots, and branded content. The royalty-free license means you won't owe ongoing fees each time the ad runs. Always ensure the song was generated while your paid subscription was active, and review the Terms of Service for any content-specific restrictions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
