---

The fastest way to understand what Suno can do is to make something. This guide walks you from a blank browser tab to a finished, playable song in six steps. No account setup friction, no music theory — just you, a text prompt, and the result.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Go to [suno.com](https://suno.com) and click **Sign Up**. You can register with a Google, Apple, Discord, or Microsoft account — no credit card required.

    Prefer to work on your phone? Download the **Suno app** from the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com) or [Google Play](https://play.google.com) and sign in there instead. Your library and credits sync across all devices.

    <Note>
      Free accounts receive **50 credits every day**, automatically renewed at midnight. That's enough to generate 10 song variations (2 per generation) each day at no cost.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate to Create">
    Once you're logged in, click **Create** in the left-hand sidebar. This opens the main generation panel where you'll write your prompt and configure your song.

    You'll see two input modes:

    - **Simple** — a single prompt field for a quick description.
    - **Custom** — separate fields for style, lyrics, and title, giving you more direct control over the output.

    Start with **Simple** for your first song.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write your prompt">
    Type a description of the song you want to hear. The more specific you are, the closer the result will be to your vision.

    A strong prompt includes:

    - **Genre or style** — indie rock, lo-fi hip-hop, jazz ballad, synth-pop
    - **Mood or energy** — upbeat, melancholic, triumphant, eerie
    - **Instruments** — driving drums, fingerpicked acoustic guitar, warm Rhodes piano
    - **Lyrical theme** — what the song is about

    **Example prompt:**
    > *"An upbeat indie rock song about a road trip across the American Southwest, electric guitar riff, driving drums, optimistic lyrics about freedom and open highways"*

    <Tip>
      Don't overthink the first attempt. Generate a version, listen to what Suno produces, then adjust your prompt based on what's close and what needs changing. Iteration is the fastest path to a great result.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Create and wait">
    Hit the **Create** button. Suno sends your prompt to its AI model and begins generating your song. On a standard connection this takes **under a minute**.

    When generation is complete, Suno returns **two distinct song variations** — same prompt, different interpretations. Both appear in the creation panel with a waveform player, so you can audition them immediately.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Listen and save your favourite">
    Play both versions in full. You'll often find that each one takes a different angle on your prompt — different chord progressions, vocal styles, or structural choices.

    When you've found the one you prefer:

    1. Click the **heart icon** (Like) to save it to your **Library**.
    2. Open your Library from the sidebar to find it any time.
    3. Click the **three-dot menu** on the song card to download the audio, copy a share link, or add the song to a playlist.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Extend or remix in the Song Editor">
    Your song doesn't have to stay exactly as generated. Click the song card to open it, then select **Edit in Song Editor** to start shaping the track.

    From the Song Editor you can:

    - **Extend** the song by generating a new section after the current ending.
    - **Add sections** — verse, chorus, bridge, outro — and generate each individually.
    - **Replace** a section you don't like without touching the rest of the track.
    - **Reorder or crop** sections to tighten the structure.

    When you're done editing, download the final mix or share it directly from the editor.

    <Note>
      The Song Editor is available on all plans, but some advanced editing features — such as stem extraction and Suno Studio — require a **Pro** or **Premier** subscription. See [Plans & Credits](/plans-and-credits) for details.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
