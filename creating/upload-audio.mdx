Upload Audio lets you bring the sounds you already have into Suno's creative process. Whether you want to transform a rough voice memo into a full song, use a chord progression you recorded as a style reference, or load your own voice for a Persona, this feature bridges the gap between what you've made and what Suno can build on top of it.

## Upload Limits

| Plan | Max Upload Length |
|---|---|
| Free | 8 minutes |
| Pro | 30 minutes |
| Premier | 30 minutes |

**Supported formats:** MP3, WAV, and other common audio formats (AAC, FLAC, OGG, M4A).

<Note>
  If your file exceeds the upload limit for your plan, trim it before uploading or consider upgrading to Pro or Premier for the full 30-minute allowance.
</Note>

## How to Upload

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Create panel">
    Go to the Create page. You'll find the **Upload Audio** button below the prompt field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your file">
    Click **Upload Audio** to open a file picker and select your audio file, or simply **drag and drop** your file directly onto the Create panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for processing">
    Suno analyzes your upload — this usually takes a few seconds for short clips and up to a minute for longer files.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose how to use it">
    Once processed, select your intended use case: **Inspo** (style reference), **Remix**, **Voice** (Persona creation), or **Studio** (Premier only). Then complete your prompt or lyrics and click **Create**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Use Cases

### 1. Style Reference (Inspo)

Use your uploaded audio as a stylistic inspiration for a new generation. Suno analyzes the tonal character, tempo, instrumentation, and production style of your file — then applies those qualities to a brand-new song based on your prompt.

This is ideal when you have:
- A reference track in a style you want to replicate
- A demo recording whose vibe you want to capture
- An instrumental loop or beat you'd like Suno to build on stylistically

```text Example prompt with style reference
Use the uploaded recording as the style reference.
Write a new song about late summer — nostalgic, bittersweet.
```

<Tip>
  Short, focused clips (30–90 seconds) work best for style references. A clip that clearly establishes the genre and mood gives Suno a stronger signal than a full 4-minute song.
</Tip>

### 2. Remix

Let Suno transform your audio into something new. In Remix mode, Suno takes your uploaded audio as a starting point and regenerates it with new production, arrangement, or vocal style based on your prompt. The result shares DNA with your original but is reimagined through Suno's generation engine.

Great for:
- Turning a raw demo into a polished production
- Switching a song from one genre to another
- Adding instrumentation or production to a sparse recording

### 3. Voice Upload for Persona Creation (Pro & Premier)

<Note>
  Voice uploads for Persona creation require a **Pro or Premier** subscription.
</Note>

Upload a recording of your own voice to create a **Persona** — a reusable vocal identity that Suno applies to any song you generate. Your uploaded voice becomes the "singer" in future generations.

For the best Persona quality:
- Record in a quiet space with minimal background noise
- Use a decent microphone — even a modern smartphone works
- Avoid heavy reverb, compression, or processing on the recording
- Record at a natural speaking or singing pitch — no need to perform dramatically
- Aim for 30–120 seconds of clean vocal content

See the [Voices & Styles guide](/creating/voices-and-styles) for step-by-step Persona creation instructions.

### 4. Studio Import (Premier)

<Note>
  Studio import requires a **Premier** subscription.
</Note>

Premier users can import uploaded audio directly into the **Suno Studio** timeline. This lets you place your audio on a track, layer it with Suno-generated stems, extend it with AI-generated sections, or use it as a reference while editing in Studio's multitrack view.

Studio import is the most powerful workflow for producers who want to blend their own recordings with Suno's generation capabilities.

## Tips for Best Results

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Use clean, dry audio" icon="waveform">
    High-quality, unprocessed audio gives Suno the clearest signal to work with. Heavy reverb, clipping, or background noise can confuse the analysis.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Shorter is better for style refs" icon="scissors">
    For Inspo and style references, a tight 30–90 second clip that clearly establishes the genre and mood outperforms a longer, genre-blending full track.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Label your uploads" icon="tag">
    Give uploads descriptive filenames before uploading — it makes it easier to identify them when you're working across multiple projects.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Try multiple approaches" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Upload the same audio as both an Inspo reference and a Remix source to compare how each workflow transforms it. The results can be surprisingly different.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  Do not upload audio that you do not own or do not have the rights to use. Uploading copyrighted material without authorization may violate Suno's [Terms of Service](https://suno.com/terms) and applicable copyright law. Only upload recordings you created yourself or audio for which you hold the appropriate license.
</Warning>
