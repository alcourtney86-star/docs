Custom Mode hands you the steering wheel. Instead of describing a song and letting Suno decide the words, you write the lyrics yourself, define the style, and name the track — giving you precise control over every section of the song. It's the best way to bring a fully-formed creative vision to life.

## Enabling Custom Mode

Toggle **Custom** at the top of the Create panel. The prompt field expands into three separate fields: **Lyrics**, **Style**, and **Title**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Create panel">
    Navigate to the Create page and locate the prompt area at the top of the screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle Custom Mode on">
    Click the **Custom** toggle. The single prompt field splits into Lyrics, Style, and Title fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in your details">
    Write your lyrics, describe your style, and give your song a title. Then click **Create** to generate two variations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Custom Mode generations cost the same as standard generations — **5 credits** per run, producing 2 song variations.
</Note>

## The Three Fields

### Lyrics

This is where you write the words Suno will sing. Use **structural tags** in square brackets to tell Suno how to treat each section. Tags are case-insensitive — `[Chorus]`, `[chorus]`, and `[CHORUS]` all work the same way.

### Style

Describe the sonic character of the song in plain language. Think of this as a condensed version of a standard Suno prompt — genre, subgenre, vocal style, key instruments, and energy level. **Keep it under 120 characters** for best results; longer descriptions can dilute the signal.

### Title

Give your song a name. The title can influence the overall tone and lyrical theme, so choose something that reflects the mood you're going for.

## Structural Tags

Tags tell Suno where one section ends and another begins, and how to arrange the music around your lyrics.

| Tag | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `[Intro]` | Opening instrumental or scene-setting section |
| `[Verse]` | Main storytelling section, typically lower energy |
| `[Pre-Chorus]` | Build-up before the chorus hits |
| `[Chorus]` | High-energy, hook-driven section — the memorable part |
| `[Bridge]` | Contrasting section that breaks the verse-chorus pattern |
| `[Outro]` | Closing section, often a fade or final statement |
| `[Instrumental]` | Signals a section with no vocals — music only |
| `[Break]` | A brief rhythmic or melodic pause |
| `[Hook]` | A short, catchy repeated phrase |
| `[Solo]` | Instrumental solo (great for guitar, piano, or sax solos) |

<Tip>
  You can stack multiple tags on a single section — for example, `[Verse][Quiet]` or `[Chorus][Intense]` — to add expressive direction to a structural marker.
</Tip>

## Complete Song Structure Example

Here's a full example of a pop song written in Custom Mode.

<CodeGroup>

```text Lyrics
[Intro]

[Verse]
Empty coffee cups line the windowsill
The morning light is cold and standing still
I wrote your name in the dust on the floor
Then I swept it away like I've done before

[Pre-Chorus]
I keep saying I'm fine
But my hands give me away

[Chorus]
Every time I hear that song
I'm back where I don't belong
Standing in the kitchen light
Missing you at 2 AM tonight

[Verse]
Your jacket's still hanging by the door
I can't decide what I'm keeping it for
The plants you left are barely holding on
Just like me since you've been gone

[Pre-Chorus]
I keep saying I'm fine
But my eyes give me away

[Chorus]
Every time I hear that song
I'm back where I don't belong
Standing in the kitchen light
Missing you at 2 AM tonight

[Bridge]
Maybe someday I'll move on
Maybe someday I'll be strong
But tonight I'm letting it hurt
Every word, every verse

[Chorus]
Every time I hear that song
I'm back where I don't belong
Standing in the kitchen light
Missing you at 2 AM tonight

[Outro]
Missing you at 2 AM...
```

```text Style
indie pop, melancholic, female vocal, fingerpicked acoustic guitar, soft drums, emotional
```

```text Title
2 AM Tonight
```

</CodeGroup>

## Style Field Tips

The Style field is one of the most powerful levers in Custom Mode. Follow this formula for consistent results:

**`[genre] + [subgenre or era] + [vocal style] + [key instruments] + [energy/mood]`**

```text
dark folk, Americana, male baritone, banjo and fiddle, slow and haunting
```

```text
90s R&B, smooth soul, female vocal, lush strings, mid-tempo
```

```text
heavy metal, thrash, no vocals, distorted guitar, double bass drum, aggressive
```

```text
lo-fi jazz, late night, female breathy vocal, upright bass, muted trumpet
```

<Warning>
  Keep your Style field under **120 characters**. Beyond that, Suno may deprioritize some descriptors. If you have a lot to specify, put the most important elements first.
</Warning>

## Creating Instrumental Tracks

To generate a fully instrumental song in Custom Mode, you have two options:

**Option 1 — Leave the Lyrics field empty.** Suno will generate music without any vocals.

**Option 2 — Use `[Instrumental]` tags throughout your lyrics field.** This gives you finer control, letting you label specific sections as instrumental while keeping others available for vocals.

```text
[Intro][Instrumental]

[Verse]
Walk with me through the fallen leaves...

[Chorus]
We were golden, we were gold...

[Solo][Instrumental]

[Outro][Instrumental]
```

<Tip>
  Pair the `[Solo]` tag with a specific instrument in the Style field — e.g., `electric guitar solo` — to get a true shredding moment right where you want it.
</Tip>

## Tips for Better Custom Mode Results

- **Match your lyrics to the style.** Dense, multi-syllable lyrics work well in rap and folk; shorter, punchy lines suit pop and rock choruses.
- **Don't over-tag.** One structural tag per section is usually enough. Stacking too many tags can confuse the model.
- **Use repetition intentionally.** Repeating a chorus block tells Suno it's a recurring hook — the model will treat it with more musical weight.
- **Leave breathing room.** Short line breaks between sections help Suno parse your structure cleanly.
- **Iterate.** Generate two variations, keep the one you prefer, then extend or re-record individual sections in the Song Editor.
