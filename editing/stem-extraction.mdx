Stem extraction separates a mixed song into its individual component tracks — for example, pulling out the vocal layer from the instrumental, or isolating drums, bass, and guitar as separate audio files. Once extracted, you can download those stems and import them into any digital audio workstation (DAW) like Ableton Live, Logic Pro, GarageBand, or FL Studio for further production, remixing, or sampling.

<Note>
  Stem extraction requires a **Pro or Premier** plan. The Free plan does not include stem access. Pro includes **Auto** and **Split from Mix** extractions; Premier adds **Advanced Split** with up to 12 individual stems.
</Note>

---

## Extraction Types

Suno offers three levels of stem separation, each suited to different workflows:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Auto" icon="scissors">
    **Pro & Premier** — Splits your song into two stems: a full **Vocals** track and a full **Instrumental** track. Ideal for karaoke backing tracks, a cappella exports, or quick vocal isolation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Split from Mix" icon="sliders">
    **Pro & Premier** — More granular separation than Auto. Breaks the mix into a wider set of layers for finer creative control.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Advanced Split" icon="waveform-lines">
    **Premier only** — Up to **12 individual stems**: vocals, drums, bass, guitar, piano, strings, and more. The most detailed separation available — ideal for full DAW productions.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to Access Get Stems

You can launch stem extraction from two places:

**From the Library or Workspace:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find your song">
    Go to your **Library** or **Workspace** and locate the song you want to extract stems from.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open More Actions">
    Click the **... (More Actions)** icon on the song card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Get Stems">
    Choose **Get Stems** from the dropdown menu.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**From the Song Editor:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Song Editor">
    Open your song and click **Edit** to enter the Song Editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the Stems icon">
    In the **top-right corner** of the Song Editor, click the **Stems icon** (the waveform/branch icon).
  </Step>
</Steps>

Both paths open the same stem extraction interface.

## Choosing an Extraction Type and Generating Stems

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select extraction type">
    In the stems panel, choose **Auto**, **Split from Mix**, or **Advanced Split** (if your plan includes it).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    Click **Generate Stems**. Suno processes the song — this typically takes between a few seconds and a couple of minutes depending on song length and extraction complexity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview stems">
    Once generated, each stem appears as its own row in the stems view. Click the **play icon** on any row to preview that stem in isolation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using the Stems Interface

After generation, the stems view gives you full playback control before you download anything:

| Control | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Solo** | Mutes all other stems so you hear only this one |
| **Mute** | Silences this stem while the others play |
| **Preview** | Plays the stem from the beginning |
| **Download** | Downloads this individual stem file |
| **Download All** | Downloads all stems as a ZIP archive |
| **Regenerate** | Runs the extraction again if results are unsatisfactory |

<Tip>
  Use **Solo** and **Mute** together to audition stem combinations before downloading — for example, solo the drums and bass together to check the low-end groove before committing to a download.
</Tip>

## Output Formats

When you download stems, you can choose from several formats depending on your intended use:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="MP3">
    Compressed audio. Smaller file sizes, suitable for quick previews, sampling, or projects where storage is a concern. Not ideal for professional production where quality is paramount.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="WAV">
    Uncompressed, full-quality audio. The standard choice for importing into a DAW. Preserves all audio detail from the Suno generation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Tempo-Locked WAV">
    A WAV file locked to the song's **average BPM**. When you import this file into a DAW with time-stretching enabled, it automatically snaps to the project's grid — no manual BPM alignment needed. This is the recommended format for DAW workflows.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="MIDI">
    A MIDI file representing the melodic or harmonic content of the stem. Import into a DAW to trigger virtual instruments, transpose the melody, or use it as a composition reference. Available for melodic stems on Premier.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="WAV + MIDI">
    Both the WAV audio file and its corresponding MIDI file bundled together. Gives you the full audio AND the underlying note data for maximum flexibility in your DAW.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  **Tempo-Locked WAVs are the best choice for most DAW imports.** They eliminate the manual work of tempo-matching and ensure every stem lines up perfectly on the grid, especially when mixing Suno stems with your own recorded tracks.
</Tip>

## Using Stems in a DAW

Once you've downloaded your stems, here's how to bring them into your production workflow:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download your stems">
    Choose your preferred format (Tempo-Locked WAV is recommended) and download individual stems or the full ZIP archive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open your DAW and create a new project">
    Launch Ableton Live, Logic Pro, FL Studio, GarageBand, or your preferred DAW. Set the project BPM to match the song's tempo (visible at the bottom of the Song Editor).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import stems to separate tracks">
    Drag each stem file onto its own audio track. If you used Tempo-Locked WAVs and your DAW has warp/flex enabled, the clips will snap to the grid automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit, mix, and produce">
    From here you have full control — process stems with effects, rearrange sections, layer new recordings, add your own instruments, or build an entirely new production around the Suno stems.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you're using Ableton Live, enable **Warp** on imported audio clips and set the Warp Mode to **Complex Pro** for best quality on melodic content (vocals, pads) or **Beats** for rhythmic content (drums, percussion).
</Tip>
