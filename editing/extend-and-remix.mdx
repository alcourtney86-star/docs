Once you've created a song you love, Suno gives you a full suite of tools to take it further — extend it into a longer track, remix it into an entirely new style, create a vocal cover, adjust its speed, or let Suno co-write alongside you. These tools work on any song in your Library and are accessible from the Song Editor or the More Actions menu.

<Note>
  **Extend** and basic **Remix** are available on all plans. Advanced editing options — including prompted extensions, style-specific remixes, and Cover — are available on **Pro and Premier** plans.
</Note>

---

## Extend

**Extend** appends a brand-new continuation to the end of your song, generated by Suno to flow naturally from where the track ends. Use it to turn a 30-second sketch into a full song, or a two-minute song into a three-minute radio-ready track.

### Quick Extend vs. Prompted Extension

| Method | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Quick Extend** | You want a seamless, automatic continuation with no extra input |
| **Extend with prompt** | You want to steer the direction — e.g., build to a climax, add a new instrument, or begin fading out |

### How to Extend a Song

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Song Editor">
    Go to your Library, open the song, and click **Edit** to launch the Song Editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click + at the far right of the timeline">
    At the very end of the track's waveform, click the **+** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Quick Extend or add a prompt">
    - Select **Quick Extend** to let Suno continue the song automatically.
    - Or type a prompt describing what should happen next — for example, *"an instrumental breakdown, then bring the chorus back"* or *"fade out slowly with just the piano."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview and commit">
    Suno generates candidates in the **Edits Library**. Audition each one, then click **Commit** to append your preferred extension to the timeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can extend a song multiple times. Each committed extension becomes a new section on the timeline, which you can continue extending or edit independently.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  If your extended section feels sonically disconnected from the main track, try fine-tuning the **transition boundary** — drag the boundary line on the waveform to find the smoothest join point.
</Tip>

## Remix

**Remix** creates a new interpretation of your song — keeping the core idea but rewriting it through a different style, genre, instrumentation, or mood. Unlike Replace (which swaps one section), Remix re-imagines the whole song from the ground up.

### How to Remix a Song

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open More Actions">
    In your **Library** or the **Song Editor**, click the **... (More Actions)** icon on the song you want to remix.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Remix">
    Choose **Remix** from the menu. The Remix panel opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe your changes">
    In the style or prompt field, describe the new direction. You can specify:
    - **Genre or style** — *"turn this into a reggae track"* or *"make it orchestral"*
    - **Tempo** — *"slow this down and make it more contemplative"*
    - **Key or mood** — *"shift to a minor key, darker atmosphere"*
    - **Instrumentation** — *"replace the synths with live-sounding acoustic instruments"*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate and review">
    Suno generates a remixed version. Review it, and if you want further changes, remix again — each remix is a new starting point.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Remixing works best when you give Suno a clear *contrast* from the original — for example, *"this is currently upbeat pop; turn it into a slow, smoky jazz ballad."* Vague prompts like *"make it better"* tend to produce subtle changes.
</Tip>

## Cover

**Cover** transforms your song by keeping its structure and lyrics while applying an entirely new vocal style and sonic identity. Use Cover when you want to hear your composition through a different artist lens — for example, turning an indie folk song into a powerful soul ballad, or a pop track into a baroque chamber piece.

### How to Create a Cover

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open More Actions on your song">
    In the **Library**, click **...** on the song you want to cover.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Cover">
    Choose **Cover** from the menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the new style">
    Enter a style prompt — for example, *"gospel choir, full church organ"* or *"lo-fi bedroom pop, hushed female vocals."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate and save">
    Suno generates the cover as a new song in your Library, keeping the original untouched.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adjust Speed

**Adjust Speed** changes the tempo of your existing song without regenerating it from scratch. Use it to match a song to a specific BPM, sync it to a project in your DAW, or simply see if the track breathes better faster or slower.

### How to Adjust Speed

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open More Actions on your song">
    In the **Library** or **Song Editor**, click **...** on your song.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Adjust Speed">
    Choose **Adjust Speed** from the menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the new tempo">
    Drag the tempo slider or enter a target BPM. Preview the result in real time before saving.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Confirm to save the tempo-adjusted version to your Library as a new song.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you plan to import your song into a DAW, adjusting the speed here first — then exporting a **Tempo-Locked WAV** via Stem Extraction — ensures your stems are perfectly aligned to the grid at your target BPM.
</Tip>

## Co-write with Suno

**Co-write** lets Suno actively participate in the creative process alongside you — suggesting continuations, improving lyric phrasing, or proposing structural changes based on what you've already written. Think of it as having an AI collaborator who listens to your draft and offers ideas on demand.

### What Co-write Can Do

- **Suggest continuations** — Suno proposes the next verse, chorus, or bridge based on the song's existing arc
- **Improve lyrics** — refine a line for better flow, rhyme, or emotional resonance
- **Propose structural changes** — identify sections that might work better reordered or revised

### How to Use Co-write

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your song in the Song Editor">
    Go to **Library**, open a song, and click **Edit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invoke Co-write">
    Click the **Co-write with Suno** option in the editor toolbar or in More Actions (**...**).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Suno's suggestions">
    Suno presents its suggestions — new lyrics, a continuation section, or structural notes. You can accept, reject, or iterate on each suggestion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Commit what you want">
    Accept the suggestions you like and commit them to the timeline, just like any other edit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Choosing the Right Tool

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Extend" icon="circle-plus">
    Add new material to the **end** of an existing song. Best for lengthening a track or building toward a finale.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Remix" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Re-imagine the **whole song** in a new style, genre, or mood. Best for creative exploration.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cover" icon="microphone">
    Keep structure and lyrics, change **vocals and sonic identity**. Best for genre-swapping or artist reinterpretation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Adjust Speed" icon="gauge">
    Change **tempo** without regenerating. Best for DAW sync or pacing adjustments.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
